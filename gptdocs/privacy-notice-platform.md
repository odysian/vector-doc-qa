Overview

This Privacy Notice explains how Northlight Metrics collects, uses, stores, and discloses information in connection with the Northlight Metrics platform, related support services, and customer communications. This notice is intended for account administrators, end users invited into a workspace, prospective customers who interact with sales or demo properties, and visitors to company-hosted web properties associated with the platform.

Northlight Metrics is committed to clear disclosures and practical controls. This notice summarizes the main categories of information we process, the reasons we process it, the choices available to users, and the time periods used for retention and deletion workflows. This is a synthetic notice for a fictional service environment and should be read as a platform governance document.
Who We Are

Northlight Metrics provides reporting, workspace analytics, dashboard collaboration, and usage monitoring tools to organizational customers. The company’s primary administrative mailing city is Harbor Pointe. Privacy oversight for the platform is coordinated by Elian Voss, who serves as the designated privacy lead and Data Protection Officer for this service.

Questions about this notice or a data request can be directed to the service contact alias privacy@northlight.test. Customers may also use the structured request workflow described later in this notice.
Information We Collect

We collect information in several categories depending on how a person interacts with the platform.
Account and profile information
When a workspace is created or a user is invited, we may process:
- name chosen by the user or administrator
- company or workspace affiliation
- role designation
- email address
- timezone and language preference
Usage and device information
We may collect:
- login timestamps
- browser type
- device identifiers assigned by the platform
- pages or reports viewed
- feature interactions
- error or troubleshooting logs
Customer-submitted content
Workspace administrators and authorized users may upload reports, notes, annotations, team labels, and structured datasets. Such content is processed to operate the service and provide requested features.
Support and communications data
When a user contacts support, participates in onboarding, or submits product feedback, we may store ticket details, message history, and account context needed to resolve the issue.
Why We Use Information

We use information to:
- provide and secure the service
- authenticate users and manage sessions
- generate analytics and reporting outputs
- troubleshoot issues and answer support requests
- improve product performance and usability
- meet recordkeeping and compliance needs
- communicate policy, service, or billing updates

We do not treat every category of information the same way. Different retention periods and handling workflows apply depending on the data type and the purpose of processing.
Cookie and Preference Controls

The platform and related web properties may use cookies or similar technologies for session management, security validation, remembering user settings, and measuring service usage. Users who access the company’s web interface can update their cookie choices through the preference tool.

For usability and compliance consistency, the cookie preference refresh every 180 days rule applies to the preference interface. When that period lapses, users may be prompted again to confirm or revise their preferences.

Certain essential cookies remain necessary for authentication, fraud prevention, and core session continuity. Optional analytics settings may be modified through the cookie controls when available.
Retention Periods

We aim to retain information only as long as it is needed for the purposes described in this notice, unless a longer period is required for legal, contractual, or security reasons.

Key examples include:
- account profile data: retained while the workspace remains active and for a short administrative period after closure
- support tickets: retained according to support operations policy
- workspace activity analytics: retained for operational reporting and trend analysis
- optional web analytics data: retained according to the product analytics lifecycle

Specifically, analytics data retained 13 months applies to product usage analytics that support aggregate reporting, product improvement analysis, and service performance review. After that period, such data may be deleted, aggregated further, or irreversibly de-identified depending on operational need.
Account Deletion and Request Handling

Users or administrators may request deletion of certain account data through support or the privacy request process. Depending on the requester’s role and the workspace configuration, some requests may require administrator confirmation before processing.

The standard account deletion fulfillment within 21 days target applies once the request has been validated and scoped. Some records may be retained longer when needed for fraud prevention, dispute handling, contractual reconciliation, or legal obligations, but such retained records are limited to the relevant purpose.

To submit a structured privacy request, users may use the designated workflow identified as PRIV-7. This workflow helps route requests for access, deletion, correction, or processing clarification. Questions may also be sent to privacy@northlight.test.
Age and Minor Accounts

The platform is intended for business and organizational use. It is not designed for use by young children. The minor account age floor 16 applies to self-directed account usage in supported environments unless an organization has a lawful and documented basis for a different managed-access arrangement under its own policies.

If we learn that an account was established in violation of this age requirement, we may restrict access while the situation is reviewed.
Sharing and Disclosure

We may disclose information to:
- service providers operating under contract
- infrastructure and hosting vendors
- customer-designated administrators
- legal or regulatory authorities when required
- advisers or auditors bound by confidentiality obligations

We do not disclose customer-submitted content for unrelated third-party advertising purposes in this fictional service model. Internal access to personal data is limited based on job function and business need.
Security and Governance

Northlight Metrics uses administrative, technical, and organizational measures intended to protect information from unauthorized access, misuse, or accidental loss. Access controls, environment separation, logging, and request tracking are applied according to internal policy standards. Privacy matters are reviewed under the supervision of Elian Voss and the service governance team.
