Quarterly Finance Memo

Memo code: FIN-Q2-26-04
Period: Q2 FY2026
From: Office of the CFO
CFO: Soren Pike
Summary

This memo provides the finance operating view for Q2 FY2026, including revenue outlook, spending controls, staffing constraints, and approval thresholds that will remain in force for the quarter unless superseded by a later executive directive. The purpose of the memo is to help business leaders align hiring, vendor commitments, and discretionary spending with the quarter’s performance expectations.

The quarter remains on a stable footing, but performance is slightly behind the planning model established at the start of the fiscal cycle. For that reason, finance is taking a measured approach: maintaining operational continuity while tightening selected cost controls and preserving flexibility for revenue-generating work.

This memo is issued under FIN-Q2-26-04 and should be referenced in approval discussions where a department is requesting an exception to normal budget handling.
Revenue Outlook

The approved revenue target $8.4M for the quarter remains the official benchmark. Based on booked business, pipeline conversion, renewal timing, and current collections posture, the latest internal estimate indicates an actual forecast $8.1M. This gap is manageable, but it narrows discretionary room for non-essential expansion.

The current outlook is not driven by one catastrophic factor. Instead, it reflects several smaller drags:
- slower conversion timing in two mid-market opportunities
- delayed professional services start dates
- one renewal pushed into the following quarter
- modest foreign-currency headwind in partner reporting

Leaders should continue to support revenue-generating activities, but they should avoid assuming a late-quarter rebound will fully eliminate the variance.
Expense Controls

To preserve margin discipline while revenue finishes below plan, the finance office is applying targeted controls beginning immediately.

The most visible measure is a travel cap reduction 12% against previously submitted discretionary travel assumptions. Customer-critical travel, contractual obligations, and executive-approved field engagements may continue, but internal trips that do not directly support customer delivery or closed revenue should be reconsidered.

Departments are also reminded that the software spend review threshold $9,000 remains in force. Any new software commitment, expansion, add-on module, or auto-renewal change that causes a purchase to exceed that amount must be routed for finance review before approval. Teams should not split purchases across invoices to avoid the threshold.

Routine operating costs may continue if they fit within approved department budgets and do not trigger the special review conditions described in this memo.
Hiring Guidance

A broad hiring slowdown remains in place for the quarter. The default rule is to delay net-new headcount unless the role is tied to a clear commercial or operational necessity. The main exception is the hiring freeze exception threshold >$180,000 ARR. If a hiring request directly supports an opportunity or customer segment with demonstrated annual recurring revenue above that level, the request may be escalated for exception review.

Exception requests must identify:
- the linked revenue opportunity
- estimated timing of revenue realization
- expected payback rationale
- manager and executive sponsor
- risk of delay if the role is not filled

Finance will coordinate with People Operations and the applicable executive owner before determining whether an exception should be granted.
Collections and Working Capital

Cash discipline continues to matter even in a quarter where the forecast remains solid. Teams working with billing, renewals, or customer account management should support the collections target DSO 41 days. This metric remains one of the most closely watched working-capital indicators for the quarter.

Where invoice disputes or approval delays are affecting collections, departments should escalate promptly rather than allowing unresolved items to age. Revenue quality is not evaluated solely on bookings; it is also affected by the speed and predictability of collection.
Department Planning Expectations

Business leaders should review open commitments and planned purchases with this quarter’s forecast in mind. Projects already underway may continue if they remain in scope and aligned to previously approved objectives. New discretionary initiatives should be justified more carefully.

Leaders should ask:
- does this spend improve revenue, retention, or delivery reliability?
- can the decision be delayed without material business harm?
- does the purchase cross the software spend review threshold $9,000?
- does the request depend on a hiring exception tied to >$180,000 ARR?
- is there a travel component affected by the travel cap reduction 12%?
Governance and Review

The finance office will present the quarter’s updated assumptions and controls during the scheduled board preparation cycle. The formal board review date 2026-04-18 remains on the calendar. Department heads should ensure that any major exception requests are submitted early enough to be reviewed before materials are finalized.

This quarter’s finance stance should be understood as disciplined rather than purely restrictive. The company is not in emergency contraction mode; rather, it is protecting optionality while dealing with a modest forecast gap.
Guidance from the CFO

Soren Pike has emphasized that teams should continue to invest in activities that clearly support customer success, retention, and revenue durability. At the same time, managers must avoid treating approved budgets as automatic entitlement when quarter conditions change. The approved plan is a guide, but execution must respond to current facts.

Managers should communicate the following to their teams:
- revenue remains important, but quality and collections matter too
- discretionary travel is reduced due to the travel cap reduction 12%
- large software decisions require review at $9,000
- hiring is constrained unless it supports opportunities above $180,000 ARR
- departments should support the DSO 41 days collections target
